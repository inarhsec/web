Education

My education is a combination of formal learning, industry-driven training, and continuous self-study, focused on building strong foundations in cybersecurity, networking, and information security.

Rather than limiting learning to certifications alone, I focus on understanding concepts deeply and applying them in real-world environments.

Academic Background​

I have completed my formal education with a strong interest in:

  • Computer networks
  • Information security fundamentals
  • Systems and infrastructure concepts

My academic foundation supports my practical work in network security design, security operations, and compliance.

Cybersecurity Learning Path​

I am actively learning and strengthening my skills in:

  • Cybersecurity engineering
  • Network security architecture and implementation
  • Vulnerability Assessment and Penetration Testing (VAPT)
  • Red team fundamentals and attack methodologies
  • Security monitoring and incident analysis

My learning approach is hands-on, using labs, simulations, and real infrastructure scenarios wherever possible.

Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC) Knowledge​

Alongside technical security, I study and apply security governance and compliance frameworks, including:

  • ISO 27001
  • PCI-DSS
  • NPCI and RBI security guidelines
  • CIS controls
  • NIST security frameworks

I focus on understanding how technical controls map to regulatory and audit requirements, ensuring security is both effective and compliant.

Self-Learning & Practical Exposure​

A large part of my education comes from:

  • Building home labs
  • Reviewing real audit observations
  • Studying incident reports and RCAs
  • Learning from production security issues
  • Writing notes and documentation for clarity

This allows me to bridge the gap between theory, implementation, and audit reality.

Philosophy of Learning​

Cybersecurity is a continuous journey, not a destination.

I believe:

  • Learning never stops
  • Security must evolve with threats
  • Practical understanding matters more than memorization

This page will continue to evolve as I gain deeper expertise and certifications.
